Wikipedia's Gender Gap & Feminist Interventions


This project looks at the work of feminist movements to intervene in Wikipedia's gender gap. Only about 20% of biographical articles on Wikipedia are about women. Feminist movements work to change this statistic by adding content about women to the encyclopedia through edit-a-thon events. Examining over 11,000 Wikipedia articles we identify that the movements are successful at adding content about women to Wikipedia, adding over 200 completely new entries in our sample alone. However we find that prevelant gender biases among strucutral features continue to persist suggesting the new need to be more granular in our analysis of gender gaps in digital projects.

The Citation Equity Movement


Citations are one of the few tools we have to (quantitatively) assess scholar's contribution. While imperfect measures they are often used to make crucial decisions during job searches, grant applications, and award committees as well as drive attention to certain articles and scholars over time. Yet citations are rife with bias and inequality, with women and people of color often earing fewer citations on similar levels of work. In this project, I use network analysis to understand how gender inequality develops through the citation process and how women are made less central to their disciplines.
